# CHA 11.07

Let
$$
\begin{align*}
S &= \left\{ x\in \mathbb{R} : x = \frac{n^2 + \sqrt{2}}{n}, n\in\mathbb{N} \right\} \\
&= \left\{ \frac{n^2 + \sqrt{2}}{n} \in \mathbb{R} : n\in\mathbb{N} \right\}.
\end{align*}
$$

Define $f:\mathbb{N}\to S$ by $f(n) := \frac{n^2 + \sqrt{2}}{n}$ for all $n\in\mathbb{N}$. 

- (a) List three elements that belong to $S$.
- (b) Show that $f$ is one-to-one.
- (c) Show that $f$ is onto.
- (d) Is $S$ denumerable? Justify your answer.

---

## (a) Solution

Three elements that belong to $S$ are

- $f(1) = \frac{1^2 + \sqrt{2}}{1} = 1 + \sqrt{2}$
- $f(2) = \frac{2^2 + \sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{4 + \sqrt{2}}{2}$
- $f(3) = \frac{3^2 + \sqrt{2}}{3} = \frac{9 + \sqrt{2}}{3}$

as required. $\blacktriangle$

---

## (b) Proof (one-to-one)

Assume $f(k) = f(m)$ for some $k,m\in\mathbb{N}$. Then
$$
\frac{k^2 + \sqrt{2}}{k} = \frac{m^2 + \sqrt{2}}{m}.
$$

Manipulating algebraically, the equation is reduced to

$$
\begin{align*}
0
&= \frac{k^2 + \sqrt{2}}{k} - \frac{m^2 + \sqrt{2}}{m} \\
\implies
0
&= m(k^2 + \sqrt{2}) - k(m^2 + \sqrt{2}) \\
&= mk^2 - km^2 + m\sqrt{2} - k\sqrt{2} \\
&= mk(k - m) - \sqrt{2}(k - m) \\
&= (k - m)(mk - \sqrt{2}).
\end{align*}
$$

This implies that $k = m$ or $mk = \sqrt{2}$. Since $k,m\in\mathbb{N}$, the second case is impossible. Therefore, $k = m$, and so $f$ is one-to-one. $\blacksquare$

---

## (c) Proof (onto)

Let $\tilde{x}\in S$. By definition of $S$, there exists $\tilde{n}\in\mathbb{N}$ such that $\tilde{x} = \frac{\tilde{n}^2 + \sqrt{2}}{\tilde{n}}$. In particular, we choose $n := \tilde{n}$, and we have $f(n) = f(\tilde{n}) = \frac{\tilde{n}^2 + \sqrt{2}}{\tilde{n}} = \tilde{x}$. Therefore, $f$ is onto. $\blacksquare$

---

## (d) Solution

Since $f:\mathbb{N}\to S$ is a bijection, $S$ is denumerable. $\blacktriangle$
